Diet Chart For Your 30s

5 Ingredients, 5 Minutes, 5 Lunches

You do have time for lunch! Here, healthy meals that take five minutes to whip up.

  1. Mediterranean Tuna Salad: Toss 3 ounces light tuna with 1 chopped tomato, 1/2 diced cucumber, 1 cup white beans, and 2 tablespoons Italian dressing.
  2. Almond Butter and Pear Sandwich: Spread 2 slices whole wheat bread with 1 tablespoon almond butter. Top with 1/2 small sliced pear and 1 teaspoon honey. Enjoy with a glass of nonfat milk.
  3. Tex-Mex Turkey Wrap: In a medium whole wheat tortilla, fold 3 ounces sliced turkey breast, 1/4 sliced avocado, 1/4 cup low-fat shredded cheddar cheese, and 2 tablespoons salsa.
  4. Ham and Brie Sandwich: Spread 1 tablespoon honey mustard on 2 pieces pumpernickel bread. Top with 2 ounces ham, 1 ounce Brie, lettuce, and tomato.
  5. Smoked Salmon Bagel: Spread 1 tablespoon whipped cream cheese on a whole wheat bagel. Top with 2 ounces smoked salmon, sliced red onion, and 2 teaspoons capers.

Your 30-Something Meal Plan

  1. Breakfast:Whole-wheat English muffin topped with 2 tablespoons natural peanut butter and 1/2 small sliced banana, and 1 cup calcium- and vitamin D-fortified orange juice
  2. Morning Snack: 1/2 cup Cheerios with 1/2 cup 1-percent or nonfat milk.
  3. Lunch: Curried shrimp salad (boil 10 shrimp and mix with 1 tablespoon mayonnaise and 1 teaspoon curry powder) in a whole wheat pita, and 1 cup watermelon chunks.
  4. Afternoon Snack: 6-ounce container light yogurt with 1/2 cup raspberries
  5. Dinner: Chickpea salad (toss 1/2 cup canned, drained chickpeas with 1 tablespoon Italian dressing), 4 ounces lean flank steak, grilled or broiled, topped with 2 tablespoons teriyaki sauce, 2 cups baby spinach sautéed in 1 teaspoon olive oil, and 1 medium baked sweet potato
  6. Evening Treat: 1 ounce dark chocolate
  7. Nutrition info for the day: 1,868 calories, 94g protein, 64g fat (17g sat), 243g carbs, 34g fiber

Some Important Things to Know In Your 30s

Maintaining a healthy diet helps prevent overweight and obesity, especially for 30-year-olds, who often live a busy lifestyle. According to the U.S. Department of Agriculture, 72 percent of U.S. men and 64 percent of U.S. women are overweight or obese. A study published in a 2007 edition of the “Journal of the American Dietetic Association” found that college students who were more knowledgeable about nutrition guidelines had healthier eating patterns. Appropriate diets for 30-year-olds are based on gender, activity level and weight-management goals

Calories

The USDA provides calorie recommendations for all adults, including 30-year-olds. Based on these guidelines, men ages 19 to 39 require 2,400 to 2,600 calories if they are sedentary, 2,600 to 2,800 calories if they are moderately active and about 3,000 calories per day if they are active or participate in physical activity equivalent to walking more than 3 miles per day. The USDA also estimates that women ages 19 to 39 require 1,800 to 2,000 calories if they are sedentary, 2,000 to 2,200 calories if they are moderately active and about 2,400 calories per day if they are active.

Weight loss

Overweight and obese 30-year-olds with a body mass index of 25 or above may consider a reduced-calorie diet for weight loss. BMI is a measurement calculated from weight and height and is a good indicator of body fat and chronic-disease risks. To calculate your BMI, multiply your weight in pounds by 703, divide by your height in inches and divide again by your height in inches; you can also use an online BMI calculator like the one provided by the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute. To safely and effectively lose excess body weight, the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics encourages an energy reduction of 500 to 1,00

Macronutrient Composition

A healthy diet for a 30-year-old consists of a good balance of carbohydrates, protein and fat. The Institute of Medicine recommends that adults ages 19 and older consume 45 to 65 percent of their calories from carbohydrates, 10 to 35 percent from protein and 20 to 35 percent of their daily calories from fat. The institute also encourages 30-year-old men to consume at least 130 grams of carbohydrates and 56 grams of protein each day and 20-year-old women to consume at least 130 grams of carbs and 46 grams of protein every day; pregnant and nursing women require at least 71 grams of protein each day.

Sample 2,300-Calorie Plan

A 2,300-calorie diet is often appropriate for 30-year-old men who are moderately active and some large-framed sedentary 30-year-old men. A healthy, sample USDA 2,600-calorie meal plans includes 3.5 cups of vegetables; 2 cups of fruits; 9 ounces of grains; 3 cups of dairy foods; 6.5 ounces of protein foods such as lean meats, seafood, eggs and soy products; 8 teaspoons of oils; and 362 additional calories each day.

Sample 1,800-Calorie Plan

A 1,800-calorie diet is usually appropriate for 30-year-old women who are sedentary to moderately active. A USDA sample 1,800-calorie meal plan consists of 2.5 cups of vegetables; 2 cups of fruits; 6 ounces of grains; 3 cups of dairy foods; 5.5 ounces of protein foods such as lean meats, seafood, eggs and soy products; 6 teaspoons of oils; and 258 extra calories each day.

Key Nutrients You Need To Know

Folate

It's critical for supporting a healthy pregnancy, preventing neural-tube defects and helping your body make new cells. Folate may also help reduce the risk of heart disease. Eat foods such as chickpeas, asparagus, spinach, broccoli, avocados, orange juice, and fortified whole grains to help meet your daily 400-microgram requirement.

Phytonutrients

"These compounds contain antioxidants, which slow the aging process, ward off heart disease, and prevent changes in DNA, potentially preventing the development of cancer," says Bonci. While phytonutrients come from plants, dark chocolate, red wine, and coffee are highest in them.

Iron

When you don't get enough iron, you might feel physically drained and mentally exhausted. Researchers at Penn State University found that young women who were deficient in the mineral took longer and performed worse on cognitive tasks than those who had normal levels of iron. Get your daily dose of 18 milligrams from foods such as clams, lean beef, fortified breakfast cereal, soybeans, pumpkin seeds, and skinless poultry. p>

Put your health first:

"In your 30s you start to see signs of an unhealthy lifestyle, such as diabetes or hypertension," says James O. Hill, Ph.D., director of the Center for Human Nutrition at the University of Colorado.

Make fast food healthy

Grabbing dinner on the go means you may be missing out on crucial nutrients, says Bonnie Taub-Dix, R.D., a spokesperson for the American Dietetic Association. Slave over the stove? Nah. Choose healthy convenience foods such as rotisserie chicken, shrimp cocktail, steamed dumplings, and salads and enjoy them with speedy additions from your kitchen such as whole wheat pasta, instant brown rice, and frozen veggies. (If you're looking for a quick homemade meal, try meal prepping.)

Drink to your health

Margaritas, mojitos, and cosmos can add up faster than you can say cheers. Enter light beer (110 calories in a 12-ounce bottle). It's filling, so you're less likely to want a second, and it takes a good, long time to drink.
